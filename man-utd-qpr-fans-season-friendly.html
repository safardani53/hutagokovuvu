Man Utd embarrassed as QPR fans sing 'Ole's at the wheel' in pre-season friendly

May 08, 2024
blog

MANCHESTER UNITED were mocked by Queens Park Rangers fans chanting 'Can we play you every week?' and 'Ole's at the wheel' during a stunning pre-season friendly on Saturday.

The Hoops scored three goals in six second half minutes to thump the Red Devils 4-2 at Loftus Road.

The QPR fans in the stadium were in such a jubilant mood that they began taunting Man Utd with the famous football tunes.

They first began with jovially asking them whether they could play them every week.

And the QPR supporters then went on to mock United manager Solskjaer, who had to witness his side's dreadful afternoon.

Competitively, the West London side have not beaten United in 17 matches, a run which stretches all the way back to 1992.

And while this was only a friendly, it was still a game to saviour for their loyal supporters who came out to watch their pre-season warm-up.

Man Utd got off to the perfect possible start when the returning Jesse Lingard neatly tucked away the opener after less than three minutes.

But just four minutes later Charlie Austin headed home an equaliser for the home side as the two teams went in all square at half-time.

QPR took a shock lead in the 53rd minute when Scotland international Lyndon Dykes netted in what would be a crazy few minutes for the hosts.

And it was four less than 60 seconds later as Dykes helped himself to his second of the game.

Anthony Elanga pulled one back for Solskjaer's men 17 minutes from time but it was merely a consolation as QPR eased to a sensational win over the Premier League giants.

Man Utd's defeat comes on the same day as the announcement that manager Solskjaer has signed a new three-year deal to keep him contracted at the club until 2024.

After putting pen to paper, the Norwegian said: “Everyone knows the feeling I have for this club, and I am delighted to have signed this new contract.

"It is an exciting time for Manchester United, we have built a squad with a good balance of youth and experienced players that are hungry for success.

“I have a fantastic coaching team around me, and we are all ready to take that next step on our journey.

"Manchester United wants to be winning the biggest and best trophies and that’s what we are all striving for.
