MÖTLEY Crüe's Tommy Lee quit the reunion tour mid-performance on June 16, 2022.

What happened to Mötley Crüe's Tommy Lee?

After a nearly seven-year hiatus, Mötley Crüe kicked off their reunion tour at Truist Park in Atlanta, Georgia on June 16.

Tommy Lee, 59, announced to a crowd of fans attending the well-anticipated tour that he had broken four of his ribs prior to the show.

“We did it! You did it, we did it, we’re f***ing here!” Lee told the crowd.

“OK, anyway, what I’ve got to tell you is about 14 days ago, I f***ing broke not one, not two, not three, but four f***ing ribs right here.”

“I wish I had a f***ing badass story, like me and Conor McGregor f***ing scrapping out in some f***ing bar or something, but I don’t,” he explained.

“The doctors told me, ‘No playing, bro. No playing.’ And I was like, ‘Are you f***ing high, bro? We’ve got a f***ing tour to do!’ So anyway, I ain’t sitting out for s**t.

"My boy’s [Tommy Clufetos] gonna help me out here and I’ll see you guys later. On with the goddamn show.”

Lee, however, only lasted five songs before he had to call it a night and hand over his sticks to Black Sabbath and Ozzy Osbourne's drummer.

Who is Tommy Lee?

Lee was born on October 3, 1962, and is known as the founding member of Mötley Crüe.

He has been married four times and has two children - Brandon and Dylan - with his ex-wife, Pamela Anderson.

Their relationship has become the focus of the new series, Pam & Tommy.

What is Tommy Lee's net worth?

Lee's net worth is estimated to be $70million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.

Apart from Mötley Crüe, he is also the founder of the rap-metal band Methods of Mayhem and has had a successful solo career in the past.

In 2004, Lee and the original lineup of Mötley Crüe reunited to release a double-CD anthology album called Red, White, & Crüe.
