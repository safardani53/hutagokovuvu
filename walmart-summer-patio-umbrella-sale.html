Walmart shoppers are rushing to buy $200 practical summer essential which scans at register for only

IT'S time to get your backyard ready for summer barbecues and outdoor gatherings.

No space is complete without the right amount of shade and Walmart has you covered with a deep discount on a summer essential originally priced at $199.99.

Walmart has on sale a huge outdoor patio umbrella which scans at the register for only $69.99 - that's $130 off the original price.

The Serwall rectangular patio umbrella is large enough to shade your next outdoor gathering.

It measures 6.5 feet by 10 feet.

It comes in a variety of colors including beige, cream, navy, orange, and red.

The umbrella has a push button to tilt sideways to adjust to the sunlight.

There's also a crank to raise and lower it.

It can be used either through a table or on its own with a base to weigh it down.

Keep in mind, pricing and availability will vary by store.

RAVE REVIEWS

For the heavily discounted price, people are celebrating that "it's a better quality than you'd expect."

Others described it as "sturdy fabric" and "pole is strong."

Buyers also said the winding mechanism "works very well."

Another said the ties to the umbrella have velcro closures.

Not all reviews were complimentary, as one buyer said the "thread is cheap."

Another wrote that the umbrella was a "little too tall."

WAYS TO SAVE AT WALMART

Shoppers have found the Walmart app to be a useful tool to save.

The app is free to download for iPhone and Android users.

Customers said by just scanning an item's barcode can reveal added discounts on pricing which may not be listed on the tag or online.

There's no guarantee a sales associate will honor the scanned price at checkout but it's a good idea to show them what you found when you are at the register.

Another way to save is by shopping Walmart's Great Value brand.

Many products taste similar to name brands but at a far lower price.

Great Value also offers items other than food.
