First Samsung Galaxy S20 cases reveal top-secret design of new iPhone rival

May 12, 2024
blog

SAMSUNG'S final design for the rumoured Galaxy S20 smartphone may have been revealed by a case maker.

The South Korean tech giant is due to unveil its next iPhone rival at an event on February 11 – but may have been beaten to the punch.

In the days leading up to a phone launch, the quality of leaks ramps up massively.

Many of the best leaks come from case makers, who acquire designs early to make sure they have accessories available when a phone launches.

Sometimes these case makers will reveal their cases ahead of the phone being launched, revealing its design early.

Case maker Totallee has sent designs for its Samsung Galaxy S20 cases to The Sun, potentially revealing the handset's final design.

"We have a track record of releasing correct cases pre-launch for the past 6 years," a Totallee spokesperson told The Sun.

"We're so confident that we got the cases right again that we’re offering our customers a money back guarantee on all pre-orders."

As expected, the Samsung Galaxy S20 looks set to have a slim and modern design.

Cases are offered by Totallee for three different models: the Galaxy S20, Galaxy S20+ and Galaxy S20 Ultra.

Each is larger than than the last, but the most notable difference is the number of cameras.

The S20 has three cameras, while the Galaxy S20+ upgrades this to four.

And the Galaxy S20 Ultra appears to have an extra feature called "100x Space Zoom".

It's widely rumoured that this model will offer very high levels of digital and optical zoom, so this design fits.

The bad news is that Samsung hasn't confirmed that these designs are official.

However, leaks typically become much more reliable in the days before launch.

This is especially true for case makers, whose businesses depend on their case designs being accurate.

So how do case makers get a hold of designs? It's typically a mixture of ways.

Sometimes, tech firms will provide case makers with designs in advance – so that their product has third-party accessories available from launch.

Other times, case makers will use sources inside a phone maker's supply chain, obtaining designs without official approval.

And often, case makers will simply base their designs on the most reliable leaks available at the time.

Samsung declined to comment on this story.
