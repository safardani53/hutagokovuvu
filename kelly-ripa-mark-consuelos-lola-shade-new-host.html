MARK Consuelos and Kelly Ripa have welcomed two of their kids as guests on their talk show earlier this week, but all did not go as planned.

The new Live host was left completely shocked after his daughter, Lola, threw shade at his new gig in the middle of the show.

On Tuesday, Kelly and Mark were joined by Lola, 22, and Michael, 26, to bake holiday cookies for a fun segment on their talk show.

The married hosts, who also share 20-year-old son Joaquin, watched on as their kids tried their hand at making gingerbread cookies.

As the 20-somethings took turns reading the recipe off the teleprompter and telling their parents what steps to take next, the hosts also asked their kids a few questions.

At one point, Kelly asked: “Hey guys, how do you think Dad’s doing as a host of the show?”

Mark smiled as he rolled out the cookie dough, waiting to hear their responses.

Unfortunately, Lola completely disregarded the question and abruptly returned to sharing more of the cookie recipe.

Michael, meanwhile, said that he thinks his dad is “killing it” as the new Live co-host.

Everyone seemed to be focused more on Lola’s apparent shade toward her dad, though, as the crowd erupted in shocked laughter.

Mark, too, appeared stunned as he looked up from his rolling pin toward the audience after the awkward moment.

Live with Kelly and Mark’s official Instagram shared a clip of the moment and added a teardrop falling from the new co-host’s eye to further show his surprise at his daughter’s shade.

One fan, who seemed to have trouble hearing Michael’s response, commented: “My favorite was when Kelly asked them how Dad was doing on the show and they both didn’t answer.”

After making a guest appearance on Tuesday’s show, Lola and Michael returned for some more on-air fun on Friday.

The duo sat at a large table on set alongside their parents, as well as some of the other show members and their own families.

With the year almost over, everyone took turns looking back on their favorite memories from the show.

Though everyone enjoyed being mostly all together for the event, Kelly said she was a little sad to not have all of her kids there.

The host said: "It's so nice to have everyone here for our holiday feast. We're just missing Joaquin. I wish he was here."

Kelly went on to explain that her and Mark's 20-year-old son is still at the University of Michigan, where he attends college.
