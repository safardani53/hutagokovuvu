Logic & Brittney Noell

New year, new addition! Rapper Logic and his wife, Brittney Noell, celebrated the new year with some baby news. On Jan. 1, Logic announced that the couple is expecting their second baby with an Instagram video of Noell's ultrasound. "Happy New Baby!" he wrote in the caption.

Brody Jenner & Tia Blanco

Brody Jenner is about to be a dad! "To start off this new year, we'd like to take this opportunity to wish all of our friends, family and followers health, happiness & an abundance of love. "We kept our relationship pretty private at the beginning," Blanco told the outlet in November 2022. "But we're both just so in love and it just happened naturally to share the fun things that we do and this exciting time in our life together."

Jenner revealed the sex of his baby with a Feb. 12 Instagram video. In the clip, two people dressed as giant babies battle it out until the one in the pink bib wins, and the crowd goes wild to celebrate a little girl on the way.

Diana Jenkins & Asher Monroe

This Real Housewife is about to take on motherhood again. Diana Jenkins is expecting another little one, a girl, with her fiancé Asher Moore. In addition to their first child, 2-year-old daughter Eliyanah, the baby will be welcomed by two older half-siblings. The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills alumna shares son Innis, 22, and daughter Eneya, 19, with her ex-husband Roger Jenkins.

The expecting mother shared an Instagram video of an ultrasound to update her followers on her pregnancy. In the caption, Jenkins confirmed all was going well with her bun in the oven: "What's up. It's 17 weeks and I am chilling in here," she wrote in March. "Good news. Doctor says we can fly!"

A source told PEOPLE that the final sentence of her caption refers to the former reality star's plans to have her baby across the pond, in London. The source added that Jenkins' other children were born in the British city as well.

In December 2022, PEOPLE confirmed that Jenkins and Moore were adding a new member to their blended family. An insider explained that the pair was "filled with joy" at the time of the announcement. "So far, everything is perfect and she feels great," the source said. However, because of her previous miscarriage, they noted that Jenkins was resting and "doing everything she can to protect her new blessing" as a precaution.

Carey Mulligan & Marcus Mumford

A rep for Carey Mulligan confirmed to PEOPLE that she and her husband, Mumford & Sons frontman Marcus Mumford, are expecting another baby this year. The new addition will be the duo's third child together.

Mulligan and Mumford became engaged in 2011 after a five-month romance. In April of the following year, they tied the knot in Somerset, England. The duo welcomed their first child, daughter Evelyn Grace, in September 2015 and their second, son Wilfred, in August 2017.

In November 2022, the She Said actress opened up to PEOPLE about the postpartum depression she faced after her first born arrived. Mulligan felt a real-life connection with Meghan Twohey, the New York Times reporter whom she portrayed in the movie, because of their similar entries into motherhood.

"One of the parts of the script that hit me initially the most was Megan's experience with postnatal depression," said the star. "I had a very similar experience with my first child seven years ago, and felt very alone, and very scared, and also very confused by the whole experience."

Glenda & Lumumba Roberts

"Baby Boom Boom" is arriving this summer! Amazing Race alums Glenda and Lumumba Roberts told PEOPLE that their little one is on the way, even though the odds of naturally conceiving were stacked against them.

The couple met at age 39 and tried for a baby before joining season 34 of the CBS reality show. They shared their hopes of conceiving "Baby Boom Boom" with viewers and continued trying after filming, though Glenda's previous health complications with uterine tumors and fibroids made them wary.

After Glenda underwent surgery, she was told that she would only be able to get pregnant through in-vitro fertilization. Shortly after, however, Glenda and Lumumba learned that they had successfully conceived naturally.

"I feel like it's a miracle," Glenda told PEOPLE on Jan. 24, during her second trimester.

Nikki Battiste & Dean Simpson

CBS News correspondent Nikki Battiste will soon have her hands full, because she and her husband Dean Simpson are expecting their second baby. The parents confirmed the big news to PEOPLE in March, detailing the lengthy and trying fertility journey she endured while hoping to give her first-born, 2-year-old son Beau, a little sibling.

"It was the third round [of in vitro fertilization] where we did get the one viable embryo and that was the best moment to hear that news," said Battiste, who explores the topic at length in her new three-part CBS series, Facing Fertility. "Then we did a fourth round because it would be nice to have a second embryo if the first didn't transfer. And then should we want a third child, we would've had to have done it then because I would definitely not have luck in a year or two."

After four back-to-back rounds of IVF, Battiste and her husband ended up with four embryos, only one of which was viable.

"We transferred that viable embryo in on November 1st, and then there was about a 60% chance it would stick and be successful. And so we found out 10 days later, November 10th, that I was pregnant," she said. "It was extremely emotional and such a relief. And in a lot of ways, a miracle because I was just about to turn 43 and I had been diagnosed with diminished ovarian reserve."

"We are grateful. We feel extraordinarily lucky that we will have two children," she added. "And our son is very excited to have a baby sister."
