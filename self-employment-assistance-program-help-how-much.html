What is the self-employment assistance program and how much can I get?

June 09, 2024
blog

UNEMPLOYED workers can get support worth hundreds of dollars a week for starting their own business.

Known as the Self-Employment Assistance Program (SEAP), we explain who's eligible and how to claim.

It comes as unemployment benefits ended for millions of Americans over the Labor Day weekend.

Three major Covid programs by the federal government came to an end, including a weekly boost of $300.

Whether you're struggling to find a job or not, you may want to consider claiming support under SEAP instead.

What is the Self-Employment Assistance Program?

SEAP allows jobless workers who meet certain requirements to start their own business while collecting unemployment insurance benefits.

States aren't required to take part but the Department of Labor provides funds for those that do.

As of August 30 this year, it was available in Delaware, Mississippi, New Hampshire, New York, and Oregon, according to the Department of Labor.

In other words, you're not guaranteed to get the help as it depends on where you live.

If you claim, you'll get a weekly allowance to support while you're getting your business off the ground.

Who is eligible?

In order to get the support, you must generally be eligible to receive regular unemployment insurance (UI) benefits.

This is a temporary benefit that lasts for up to 26 weeks, depending on the state, while you look for a new job.

If you've been permanently laid off from previous jobs and you're likely to exhaust regular unemployment benefits, you're eligible for SEAP.

Whether you're likely to use up all your regular unemployment benefits is determined by your state's profiling system.

You may be eligible even if you're taking part in self-employment activities full-time, including entrepreneurial training and business counseling.

The criteria does vary between states though, and in New York, for example, your proposed business must meet five criteria for approval:

  • Locating your proposed business in New York State
  • Willing to work full-time to launch your business
  • Planning to be an active owner of your business and not a silent partner in a business partnership
  • A first-time business owner and operator of the proposed type of business. You can't have previously owned or operated a business of similar nature.
  • Prepared with a clear business idea

How much you can get

SEA allowances are the same weekly amounts as a worker's regular unemployment insurance benefits.

This is a portion of your previous wage, with the percentage depending on your local state.

Of the states mentioned above, Oregon pays the highest maximum weekly benefit of $673 compared to a minimum of $157.

Meanwhile, New York offers up to $504, New Hampshire up to $427, Delaware up to $400 and Mississippi up to $235.

How to apply

You should contact the State Unemployment Insurance agency as soon as possible after becoming unemployed.

At the time you file your claim, ask whether there's a SEAP in your state.
