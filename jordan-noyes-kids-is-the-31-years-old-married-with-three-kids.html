Jordan Noyes Kids: Is The 31 Years Old Married With Three Kids?

Yes, you read that right. The Colorado State kicker Jordan Noyes is 31 years old and is married with 3 kids.

You may have encountered a trending topic on Twitter regarding a college football player named Jordan, who is a married father of three and a standout athlete.

This remarkable individual is none other than the 5-foot-11-inch kicker for Colorado State University.

Jordan was born on August 3, 1992, in Dartford, Kent, England, which adds an interesting international flavor to his college football journey.

His birthplace in England sets him apart from most college football players who typically hail from the United States.

One notable fact about Jordan is that he ranks as the second-oldest player in college football as of 2023.

He only trails behind ECU’s Luke Larsen, who was born on July 18, 1992, making them both part of a select group of mature players in the college football scene.

Jordan’s strong family support has been a driving force behind his success. His parents, David and Linda, have been his pillars of strength throughout his life.

Jordan’s upbringing was shared with two sisters and a brother named Ryan, who forged strong family bonds together.

As Jordan continues to excel in college football, his story is an inspiring example of determination, family support, and the international appeal of this beloved American sport.

Jordan Noyes Kids

Jordan Thomas Noyes, the son of David and Linda Noyes, comes from a close-knit family.

He is married to the former Jordan Tulip.

Their love story has blossomed into a beautiful family, with three children to complete their happiness.

They have a daughter named Scottie, and adorable twins named Skye and Summer, making their household lively and joyful.

In a significant life change, Jordan, his wife Jordan, and their daughter Scottie relocated to Utah at the beginning of 2020.

Later that year, in November, they welcomed twin girls, Summer and Skye, further adding to the joy and adventure of their journey together.

One remarkable aspect of Jordan’s journey is that he was the first member of his family to move to the United States.

This decision paved the way for his unique college football experience, adding an international dimension to his athletic career.

Another fascinating connection in Jordan’s life is his cousin, who happens to be married to former Ute All-American kicker Matt Gay.

This family tie adds an exciting layer to Jordan’s football journey as he follows in the footsteps of accomplished athletes within his own extended family.

Wedding And Journey To American Football

In 2018, Jordan Noyels and his wife, Jordan Tulip, celebrated their wedding in Portugal, surrounded by their closest family and friends.

Among their guests was Matt Gay, a talented football player who had just been drafted by the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in the National Football League (NFL) and would later go on to play for the Los Angeles Rams.

The day before the wedding, a group of friends and family gathered to play football, and it was during this friendly match that Jordan and Matt recognized their strikingly similar kicking techniques and power.

This discovery sparked a conversation between the two, during which Matt suggested that Jordan send film footage of his practice sessions to the college he was currently playing for, the University of Utah.

Over the next few months, Jordan diligently recorded his practice sessions and shared the footage with Matt, who generously provided valuable tips and advice on how to enhance his kicking technique and overall skill.

This collaboration bore fruit in the summer of 2019 when Jordan had the opportunity to attend a camp at the University of Utah, aiming to earn a spot on the Utes’ football roster.

His hard work and dedication paid off, and he successfully “walked on” to the Utes’ roster.

Brief On Jordan Noyes 

Jordan, a Colorado sophomore, began his academic journey at Gad’s Hill School, an independent day school located in Rochester, Kent, England.

His diverse athletic background in England included participating in American football, soccer, rugby, and gymnastics.

Upon arriving at Colorado, Jordan walked on to the football team in the fall, where he primarily handled kickoffs in all five games during his freshman year.

He recorded an impressive 26 kickoffs, accumulating 1,649 yards with an average of 63.4 yards per kick and 11 touchbacks.

Notably, he achieved a career-high with eight kickoffs for 503 yards (62.9 yards per kick) against Washington State. His performance earned him a spot on the Pac-12 Academic Honor Roll.

In his sophomore season in 2021, Jordan saw action in 13 games. He had 69 kickoffs with an average of 61.5 yards per kick and 29 touchbacks.

Additionally, he displayed accuracy in PATs, going 21-of-21, and made two of his four field goal attempts.

In 2022, Jordan continued to make his mark, playing 12 games.

He showcased his accuracy with a 6-for-8 record on field goals and a perfect 34-for-34 on PATs.

He kicked off 50 times throughout the season, further solidifying his role on the team.

Notable moments included nine kickoffs (four touchbacks) during the Pac-12 Championship against No. 4 USC and making field goals at Florida and Arizona State.

A highlight of his season was tying the single-game program record by earning 10 points via PAT against Southern Utah.

In the season opener at Florida, he connected on field goals of 31 yards and a career-long 43 yards, showcasing his continued growth and impact on the team.
