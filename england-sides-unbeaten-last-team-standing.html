Revealed: 18 sides in England are still unbeaten... but who will be the last team standing?

June 02, 2024
blog

FROM the Premier League down to the National League there are 18 teams still unbeaten and we look at who the bookies think will remain invincible the longest.

There is at least one team in each of the top five divisions who are still unbeaten, with some already nine league games into the campaign.

In the top flight Manchester City are one of four teams and they are just 2/1 with Betfair to stay unbeaten the longest.

Liverpool are 10/1 to hold onto their record longer than anyone else in the country, while Chelsea are a huge 40/1 to do so.

The bookies don't back Watford to maintain their stunning start to the season and they are a huge 250/1 in the market to keep their record.

Championship sides Leeds and Middlesbrough are tied at 18/1 as they both sit joint top of the second-tier of English football.

Sunderland are 7/2 to cling onto their resilient record the longest as they fight for promotion in League One.

League One boasts five unbeaten sides, while League Two still has four teams yet to suffer defeat.

Leyton Orient lead National League rivals Harrogate Town in the bookies eyes as they fight for League football.

Betfair Spokesperson Katie Baylis said: “There are currently 18 unbeaten teams in English football's top five tiers this season with all of them of course keen to keep that record going for as long as possible.

"As you might expect we have Manchester City as the favourites to stay unbeaten the longest, followed by Sunderland in League One at 7/2, Lincoln in League Two at 11/2 and Liverpool at 10/1.

"Despite Watford’s excellent start to the season, which included their win over Spurs last time out, we have them as the 250/1 outsiders to stay unbeaten the longest, with their next opponent Manchester United, who we think will probably have the edge despite their less than impressive start to the season.”
