GWEN Stefani has no team members going to the finale after her final contestant was eliminated.

During Tuesday's live episode, the nine remaining singers dwindled to only five as host Carson Daly revealed who will be performing on The Voice season finale next week.

Lila Forde of Team Legend, Huntley of Team Niall, Ruby Leigh of Team Reba, Mara Justine of Team Niall, and the fan instant save winner Jacquie Roar of Team Reba will be competing to win the title.

Gwen's only remaining singer, Bias, was eliminated.

"Mac & Nini have to fight for a spot while RUBY made it?? I’m convinced this is rigged once again," one fan tweeted.

NOT A FAVORITE?

Throughout the season, fans have shared their honest opinions on Gwen's ability to coach - and many of the comments were negative.

On Monday's episode, fans ripped the Hollaback Girl singer for her "poor coaching" as they watched Bias perform God Bless the Broken Road by Rascal Flatts.

While the judges, including Gwen, went wild for the performance, viewers watching at home weren't impressed.

Several critics didn't like the way he sounded.

One posted on X: "So how many more country classic songs is he going to destroy with her coaching before he is voted off? Another POP star in country in the making."

"Bias was off pitch quite often in that performance of Bless the Broken Road. Oddly pitch problems are almost the norm with Gwen's contestants," a second person commented.

A third hater commented: "Bias is dive bar level. I'm honestly mad about the talent that's gone home before him."

"If I'm being so serious...if Bias and Jordan don't both go home tomorrow ONE of them better go home. It's easy to butcher a Rascal Flatts song but its VERY hard to f**k up a Blake Shelton song...but ofc she made it her mission to do so," someone else reacted with.

A fifth person critiqued: "Can someone tell me why Bias is still here?"

MAKING PREDICTIONS

Last week, fans had a feeling that Gwen's team would be the first to go after discussing their predictions on social media.

"Even Gwen can't bs her way out of this one," one user commented on an online thread where viewers discussed the night's outcome.

"Gwen isn’t good at picking. I think she has picked him solely off of his stage presence," another wrote, talking about Bias.

"Gwen’s team is so disappointing," a third remarked.

"Wouldn’t it be funny if all of Gwen’s team was eliminated," a fourth added, while another thought the decision was "obvious."
