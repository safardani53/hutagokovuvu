ENGLAND hero Stuart Broad left cricket fans devastated at his shock retirement.

However, they may yet be able to celebrate with the 37-year-old fast bowler after his award-winning country pub was rebuilt.

The Tap & Run pub in Upper Broughton, on the Nottinghamshire-Leicestershire border, was ravaged by a fire in the early hours of June 11 last year after laundry ignited in a tumble-dryer.

Pictures showed the pub engulfed in flames with black smoke billowing from the building.

Co-owner and ex-team-mate Harry Gurney told SunSport Broad was "absolutely gutted" and "heartbroken" to see the pub in ruins.

It left the entire first and second floor burnt out and the roof destroyed, with the ground floor needing to be stripped down to brick as a result of the smoke and water damage inflicted.
