Professional Career and ESPN- Who Is Commentating MNF?

Talking about his career, Joe started his broadcasting career at the KXAS-TV, an NBC affiliate in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex.

Then in 1994, Tessitore joined WRGB in Albany, New York, before joining WFSB in Harford, Connecticut, the year later.

Likewise, he worked relentlessly there before taking over the primary anchor sports role in 1997. But many will admit that working at ESPN is what brought Joe enormous exposure and fame.

Since February 2002, Tessitore began commenting on ESPN boxing as a part of Tuesday Night Fights and Friday Night Fights. Soon, the newbie appeared on ESPN College Football and college basketball broadcasts.

Because of his wide range of coverage of sporting events, Joe is featured as the contributor for ESPN.com. Not to mention, he is highly praised for his broadcasting style.

Fans specifically love his distinctive style, commanding voice, and penchant for fist-clenching moments on-air.

Similarly, many media has deemed his style as “the Tess effect.”

Sadly, not all are a fan of him, and it was one of the reasons why Joe was removed from Monday Night Football before the 2020 season.

“Tessitore is a merchant of schmaltz…Cliches are a given in football announcing, but few commentators imbue NFL banalities with the portentous sentimentality that Tessitore brings to bear.”

Awards and Achievements As a Broadcaster
