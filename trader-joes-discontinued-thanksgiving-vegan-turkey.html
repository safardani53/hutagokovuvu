TRADER Joe’s has just confirmed that it has said sayonara to its popular Thanksgiving item.

Shoppers are reeling after realizing Trader Joe’s has discontinued its Vegan TURKEY-LESS Stuffed Roast with Gravy.

A Trader Joe's employee confirmed to The U.S. Sun via a phone call that has decided to get rid of the item this year.

One Reddit user revealed that the Thanksgiving turkey alternative “wasn’t meeting expectations.”

While others suggested the recipe changed and was not as good as the original.

Regardless of the many theories, shoppers are devastated taking to X calling the move “heartbreaking.”

PRODUCT DETAILS

The Vegan TURKEY-LESS Stuffed Roast with Gravy was for those who want a break from the bird.

Each roast came frozen, breaded, and even had its own vegan gravy.

Made from mostly wheat gluten, the vegan roast was stuffed with wild rice, cranberries, celery, and onions.

The breading was seasoned with herbs and red pepper flakes, adding texture and taste to each bite.

To eat, shoppers would just heat it up, slice it into medallions, and serve it with a side of its hot, vegan gravy.

OTHER OPTIONS

While shoppers may not be able to score their favorite vegan turkey from Trader Joe's, other retailers are carrying their own version.

Target sells a 35.2-ounce vegan turkey for just $15.

Made by the brand Gardein, the turkey is vegan, dairy-free, and Kosher.

It comes with cranberry and sage stuffing and homestyle gravy.

Additionally, it's ready to eat in just 60 minutes and serves five.

Amazon is also selling a frozen Tofurky for just $13.

The Tofurky Roast is pre-cooked and made from tofu-wheat protein blend, created by the Tofurky brand.

It's stuffed with wild rice and whole wheat bread crumb stuffing and is said to carve cuts like real turkey.

Created in 1995, the Tofurky Roasts was the original and best-selling alternative to turkey.
