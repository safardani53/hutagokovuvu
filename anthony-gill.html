Early Life, Overcoming Childhood Adversity & Education

Early Life

The Washington Wizards power forward Anthony Gill was born on 17 October 1992 in High Point, North California, United States.

Born to his father, Anthony Gill Sr., and mother, Sandi Summers, he grew up with his brother Dayton and two sisters, Kaytlyn & Nichole.

Overcoming Childhood Adversity

According to Gill, when it comes to overcoming difficulties, there aren’t many who have had to go through as much as him.

He was born with facial paralysis. When his mother was in labor, the doctor used forceps to pull him out of the womb, damaging nerves on one side of his face.

Consequently, it took months for the damage to go away and for the muscles on his face to function properly.

This happened without his proper knowledge, but his parents’ divorce hurt him real bad as he was 12 years old at the time.

To make matters worse, his mother was diagnosed with breast cancer the summer before his senior year of high school.

At one point, it seemed like she made it through the disease, but it came back during his freshman year of college.

The witty guy you see on the court has gone through enormous adversity in life and has overcome everything in his power.

Education

Anthony Gill initially played for the South Carolina Gamecocks for a season before being transferred to the University of Virginia.

He did receive interest from North Carolina and Ohio State, but he ultimately chose Virginia.

Gill redshirted his sophomore season, and in his junior season, coming off the bench, he helped the team to win both the ACC regular season and tournament titles.

Career

Despite having an outstanding collegiate career, Gill went undrafted in the 2016 NBA Draft, so he signed with MHP Riesen Ludwigsburg in Germany.

However, he failed the medical exam, so he left the team before the season’s start and then signed with Turkish team Yeşilgiresun Belediye of the Basketbol Süper Ligi.

After a season in Turkey, Anthony signed a contract with the Charlotte Hornets of the NBA to play in the 2017 NBA Summer League, joining Dwayne Bacon, Tyrell Corbin & Treveon Graham.

In 2017, he inked a deal with Russian team Khimki of the VTB United League & played for two seasons before leaving on August 3, 2020.

Gill, who was yet to NBA debut then, saw his wish became a reality when he signed a two-year contract with the Wizards on November 30, 2020.

He scored three points in his NBA debut on December 29, 2020, in a loss against Chicago Bulls.

Anthony’s two-year extension with the Wizards on July 1, 2022, effective throughout the 2023-24 season.

With the Wizards, the NBA power forward made his career-high 18 points in a 100-97 win against the Bulls on January 11, 2023.

Father OF Three With Wife Jenna

Wife

The Wizards forward, Anthony Gill, has been married to his high school sweetheart Jenna Jamil since April 8, 2016.

The pair met in high school in Charlotte.

Interestingly, Jenna’s sister actually had a crush on Gill, and she wanted Jenna to find out who he was, but the two of them ended up together.

They began communicating via Facebook. The two still remember their first date but has two different stories.

According to Jenna they went to a French restaurant and then went for a movie. But Gill says it was an Italian restaurant and that Jenna’s mother came along too.

Wherever their initial meeting was, both have their own beautiful version of the story, which brings a big smile to their face every time they try to recollect the memory.

The wonderful young couple waited until after the NCAA tournament to get married.

Interestingly, Gill wore a pair of Air Jordans at the ceremony while Jenna wore Nike Rosche.

The reason that came up is because the two are obsessed with shoes.

Gill’s teammates Davon Hall, Darius Thompson, London Parrantes, and Malcolm Brogdon were groomsmen.

Together, the pair share three children: one daughter (the eldest one) named Andi Raine (b. September 7, 2017) and two sons: Tripp (b. May 2, 2019) & Asher Royal (b. April 27, 2021).

Net Worth & Contracts

Net Worth

As of December 2023, Anthony Gill has an estimated net worth between $1 and $3 million.

Gill earns well through his NBA contracts and brand endorsement deals.

As of this writing, the forward has earned $4,252,381 in NBA salaries and incentives, and by the time his ongoing contract with the Wizards ends, he will have made $6.24 million.

Before NBA, too, he used to earn a decent sum in the international leagues.

During his time with the Khimki, his 2018 extension made him one of the highest paid players in Europe at the time.

Contracts

On November 30, 2020, Gill signed a two-year $2.4 million contract with the Wizards.

It earned him a salary of $898K in the 2020-21 season & $1.517 million for the 2021 season.

Similarly, on June 27, 2022, the team extended a $2.01 million qualifying offer to Gill, which made him a restricted-free agent.

On July 1, 2022, Gill and Wizards agreed on a fully guaranteed two-year $3.83 million deal, with an annual average salary of $1,916,664.

Thanks to the deal, Anthony Gill’s salary for the 2022-23 season is $1.836 million, which goes up to $1.997 million in the 2023-24 season.
